Music is the Universal Language to Convey Emotions.

Emotions are felt by every single person allthroughout the world. It is what shapes our being, we use it as a vessel to convey our desires and thoughts. Although it is true that everybody feels emotions, not every one can communicate "I'm sad" or "I feel very happy" with just words to anyone else. Sure you can use facial recognition, but it doesn't really go into the intricate intesnities and magnitude of the felt emotion. Music, however, is a great medium to communicate feelings, ambiance, and essence to whoever hears them. We hear music from different cultures from different parts of the world. Consider a radnom culture from anywhere in the world. Even if one had not experienced anything from that culture, you can still capture the emotion a musician from said culture is trying to convey. Here's an example!


Gregorian Chant on channel Veritas on YouTube

Listening to the first few seconds and you already get the feeling of a holy church with a link to the divines. This is actually a catholic church chant from the early time of the 11th century. Not only is this from a different world, but an entirely different time as well, yet you still get the essence it is trying to convey.
